002 · Tools & Method

The ODIX Method — color grading built on principles, not presets

Every tool in the DCTL suite is built around a specific problem in the color grading workflow — not to add a look, but to solve a technical constraint. The method is the system behind the tools.

001 Free

Skin Anchor

Locks skin tone to a fixed hue angle across shots. Eliminates the drift that makes multicam grades look inconsistent.

DCTL Skin Tone
002 Free

Parade Match

Numerical RGB parade matching. Remove guesswork from shot matching across different cameras and lighting setups.

DCTL Shot Match
003 Free

Halation

Film halation simulation built from actual optical physics — not a blur layer. Light bleeds the way it does on film emulsion.

DCTL Film Look
004 Free

Zone Mapper

Zone-system exposure control for digital cinema. Map tonal zones explicitly instead of guessing with lift/gamma/gain.

DCTL Exposure Zone System
005 Free

Bleach BP

Bleach bypass simulation — reduced silver removal process. Contrast and desaturation without the flat, muddy look of bad presets.

DCTL Film Look Process

003 · About

Six years. One city. One craft.

I am Odbat Batsaikhan — a colorist, filmmaker, and tool developer based in Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia. I have been working professionally in color grading for six years, building systems, tools, and training that reflect global-standard craft with regional relevance.

"Your color grade isn't invisible because it's subtle.
It's invisible because it's honest."

I started NODX as a studio. Now it is a four-vertical ecosystem — creative agency, creator academy, community, and talent — built for Mongolian and Asian filmmakers who are serious about their craft. I build in public. Most of my tools are free. The method behind them is what I teach.
